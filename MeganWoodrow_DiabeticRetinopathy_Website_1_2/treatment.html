What are the Treatments?

WHAT YOU CAN DO

  • Most importantly, control your blood sugar.
  • Keep records of your blood sugar levels and your most recent hemoglobin A1C blood test result to help your doctor to review. You may require a close collaboration with your primary medical doctor for regular monitoring an adjustment of medications as needed.
  • You should be checked for high pressure and high cholesterol.
  • Keep getting regular eye exams.
  • If you experience some symptoms, you should contact your eye doctor who will meet you sooner.

TREATMENT

The treatment of Diabetic Retinopathy depends on the severity of the eye disease and the specific complications that become apparent. If not severe, you only will require regular monitoring.

THESE TREATMENTS ARE:
Laser Treatment:
This treatment is most commonly used for retinal diseases. It uses a focused light beam to cauterize, or sear, the retina. It usually does not hurt. Overall, it can be performed for Diabetic Retinopathy to help cause decline of the abnormal blood vessels. It also helps to slow the progression of the Diabetic Retinopathy.
Surgical Treatment:
It is usually reserved for the most severe complications.
Intravitreal Injections:
They are shots of medicine into the eye.
Newer treatments will continue to be developed.
